DraftPick

A tool for building, evaluating, and saving team compositions for your team in a structured way.

What it is and what it is used for

DraftPick helps you think about drafting and team identity: choose a composition archetype, insert champions by role, and view useful metrics (stats, synergies, gank potential), to help your team to win in ranked que or clash.

The available views

Theory

Theoretical section: explains the concepts on which Team Builder is based (archetypes, classes, synergies, metrics).

Team

Team Builder: Create and manage team compositions, including ranking and synergies.

How to use the Team Builder

1

Add your champion pool

For each player add your best champions clicking "+".

2

Select your blind-picks

Look at your full champion pool, patch's meta and competitive scene to see what champions are strong blind picks.

3

Create a composition

Press “Add New Composition” and give the composition a name.

4

Choose team's archetype

Select the type (Charge, Catch, Protect, Siege, Split). This determines the identity, game plan, and win conditions.

5

Enter the champions by role

Add Top/Jungle/Mid/Bot/Support from the sample grid. You can replace a sample by clicking its icon.

6

Read results and save

Check stats, synergies (team and bot lane), and gank potential, then save your composition.

Champion Classes

  • Vanguard

    Vanguards are offensive tanks. Vanguards lead the charge for their team and specialize at bringing the action. Their explosive team fight initiation seeks to catch enemies out of position while allowing allies to follow-up to devastating effect.

  • Warden

    Wardens are defensive tanks. Wardens stand steadfast, seeking to hold the line by persistently locking down any on-comers who try to pass them. Wardens keep their allies out of harm’s way and allow them to safely deal with enemies caught in the fray.

  • Diver

    Divers are the more mobile portion of the Fighter class. Divers excel at singling out high-priority targets to blitz toward, immediately forcing those targets (and their teammates) to deal with the diver’s presence. Divers are not as durable as the tanks or juggernauts of the world, but Divers can take their fair share of punishment while bringing enough damage to be a real kill threat if left unchecked.

  • Juggernaut

    Juggernauts are melee titans who relentlessly march down the opposition and devastate those foolish enough to get within their grasp. They are the only subclass who excel at both dealing and taking significant amounts of damage, but in turn they have a tough time closing in on targets due to their low range and extremely limited mobility.

  • Assassin

    Assassins specialize in infiltrating enemy lines with their unrivaled mobility to quickly dispatch high-priority targets. Due to their mostly melee nature, Assassins must put themselves into dangerous positions in order to execute their targets. Luckily, they often have defensive tricks up their sleeves that, if used cleverly, allow them to effectively avoid incoming damage.

  • Skirmisher

    Skirmishers (also known as Duelists) aim to shred through any nearby enemy that approaches. Because Skirmishers lack high-end burst damage or reliable ways of closing in on high-priority targets, they are instead armed with situationally powerful defensive tools to survive in the fray, along with extreme sustained damage to cut down even the most durable targets.

  • Hypercarry

    Hypercarries are marksmen with the highest damage per second potential of the game. They tend to be weak in early game and scale on late. Their goal is to melt enemies in mid-late game teamfights due to their toolkit, but need to have team memebers who protect them because of their low mobility.

  • Lane-Bully

    Lane-bullies are marksmen with high lane dominance, thanks to their good damage on early game and range, their goal is to push out of lane their opponents and take advantage from early kills, towers and early objectives. Also they have higher burst damage and less dps, so tend to be less efficient in late team fights.

  • Scout

    Scouts are most well rounded and most spell reliant marksmen, they tend to have more utility then other marksmen's classes, providing vision, controls or good poke damage from their high range spells. Scouts are generally good in all pahses of the game and perform in a large variety of compositions.

  • Artillery

    Artillery mages are the masters of range, and they leverage that advantage to whittle down their opponents over time from great distances. In turn, Artillery Mages are severely punished when enemies finally succeed in closing in on them, due to their extreme fragility and limited mobility.

  • Battlemage

    Battlemages get into the middle of the fray, seeking to wreak havoc upon the entire enemy team with their overwhelming sustained area damage. Due to their relatively short (but not melee) combat ranges and the need to burn down their opponents over time, Battle Mages have significant defensive capabilities that range from sustaining endlessly to literally defying death for a short period of time.

  • Burst

    Burst mages aim to single out vulnerable targets by locking them down and following up with a devastating barrage of damage from range. They are strongest when using their full suite of spells executed perfectly to maximum effect, and most vulnerable when they cannot deliver.

  • Catcher

    Catchers specialize in locking down opponents or, in some cases, entire battlefields by creating intense zones of threat that only foolish enemies would dare wade through. Although not as reliant on their friends as Enchanters, the fragile Catchers greatly benefit from allied presence - both to deter incoming danger and to help capitalize on targets they've locked down.

  • Enchanter

    Enchanters focus on amplifying their allies' effectiveness by directly augmenting them and defending them from incoming threats. Enchanters themselves are often quite fragile and bring relatively low damage to the table, meaning they really only shine when grouped together with others.

  • CHARGE

    Key Attributes: Strong duelist, strong wave clear, disengage
    Identity: Lanes oriented
    Game Plan: Funnel resorces to split carry, take early advantages, stall team fights to let splitter to push
    Win Conditions: Split enemies apart
    Pros: Massive pressure in multiple lanes
    Cons: High risk, high macro knowledge required

  • PROTECT

    Key Attributes: Hyper scaling threat, disengage, utility
    Identity: Teamfight oriented
    Game Plan: Scale to late game, do not over aggro, force team fights at objectives when carry is on spike
    Win Conditions: 5v5 team fights, keep carry alive
    Pros: Very safe and tilting, strong against popular comps
    Cons: Must stay grouped as 5, extremely carry dependent

  • SPLIT

    Key Attributes: Strong duelist, strong wave clear, disengage
    Identity: Lanes oriented
    Game Plan: Funnel resorces to split carry, take early advantages, stall team fights to let splitter to push
    Win Conditions: Split enemies apart
    Pros: Massive pressure in multiple lanes
    Cons: High risk, high macro knowledge required

  • CATCH

    Key Attributes: Burst damage, single target hard cc, mobility
    Identity: Skirmish and Kills oriented
    Game Plan: Get advantage early game, set vision around objectives to ambush, get enemies out of position
    Win Conditions: Unfair Fights 5v4, vision denial
    Pros: Punishes mistakes/greed Cons: Weaker 5v5 fighting

  • SIEGE

    Key Attributes: Long range, tower damage, disengage
    Identity: Objectives oriented
    Game Plan: Get advantage early game, poke enemies at objectives to make them retire
    Win Conditions: Grind out opponents, objectives oriented
    Pros: Great objective control
    Cons: Required extreme patience and coordination

Hard Counters

  • SPLIT >>> PROTECT

    Protect wants to be engaged, and wants 5v5 team fights at objectives. Split negates this advantage by winning pushes and skirmishes, by splitting the opposing team, which is weak when separated, or by never engaging in combat, stalling it. Furthermore, Split tends to gain an advantage early in the game, precisely when Protect is weakest.

    TIPS Split:
    - All on the split-pusher's shoulders
    - Wave clear mid
    - Do not team fight
    - Track enemy movements
    Protect:
    - Bait fights
    - Need pushing power
    - Need to stall out split pusher
    - Baron can win or lose you game

  • PROTECT >>> CHARGE

    Charge wants to engage in 5v5 objective-based fights, but Protect wants just that and has all the tools to handle it. This translates into a matchup where the champions' mobility for engagement meets utility, damage, and resistances, making the matchup uneven.

    TIPS
    Protect:
    - Play safe early
    - Team fight late game
    - Stay grouped
    - Threaten Drake / Baron
    Charge:
    - Fight early
    - Don't fight 5v5 late
    - Split team into multiple groups
    - Pressure lanes prior to objectives

  • CHARGE >>> SIEGE

    Siege wants to stay long distances on objectives and wage a war of attrition. It doesn't want quick engagements, but a methodical demolition of enemy resistances, skill shot after skill shot. Charge prevents this by having the ability to quickly close the distance and explode the opposing team with a combination of abilities, which tends to be made up of frail champions, making it the number one counter.

    TIPS
    Charge:
    - Be decisive
    - Utilize flanks
    - Target carries
    - Get sustain items
    Siege:
    - Get advantage early
    - Tower advantage key
    - Don't get cocky
    - Ward flanks
    - Stall fights as long as possible
    - Disingage essential to winning

  • SIEGE >>> CATCH

    Catch wants to gain an early advantage, control map vision, and set up ganks on isolated and out-of-position champions. This doesn't tend to happen with Siege, as he always moves with 5 players, plays very cautious and calculated , and has much more range and disengagement tools, which negate single-target aggression. Siege also tends to have an advantage over Catch in all phases of the game, because early isn't as easily abused as other teamfight comps, and it's stronger in teamfights.

    TIPS
    Siege:
    - Be slow, methodical and deliberate
    - Should be stronger in 5v5 fights
    - Avoid face checking
    - Threaten most valuable objective possible
    Catch:
    - Vision control/denial essential
    - Pressure multiple objectives
    - Must be decisive
    - Look for picks in rotations

  • CATCH >>> SPLIT

    Split wants to separate, apply pressure on different lanes, and stall fights while the split-pusher destroys structures, forcing opponents to deploy two or three champions to deal with him, allowing the rest of the team to take objectives with a numerical advantage. Catch, specializing in single-target aggression, doesn't allow this, as he has all the tools to win 5v4 fights, and is capable of quickly killing the split-pusher.

    TIPS
    Catch:
    - Must shut down split pusher
    - Use summoner spells to get kills
    - Bait their team into following
    Split:
    - Split-pusher needs to be able to 1v2
    - Hide allies near split-pusher
    - Pressure objectives when they go for a pick

Soft Counters

  • SPLIT > SIEGE

    Split manages Siege's early-game strength, and has the advantage in the late game that it isn't interested in taking objectives quickly, but in stalling them so the split-pusher can do its job. And Split, unlike other Team Fight comps, doesn't have the tools to force an objective, nor to easily get rid of the split-pusher. Siege still has the opportunity to challenge Split's early game, and it must do so, to gain an advantage that could become unmanageable for its opponents.

    TIPS
    Split:
    - Funnel resources into split-pusher
    - Itemize for wave clear and sustain
    - If you're even, you're winning
    Siege:
    - Early game essential
    - Need to push, take lots of plates & get tower advantage
    - Get rift herald
    - Keep them on their side of the map

  • SIEGE > PROTECT

    Both teams want to win 5v5 objectives, but Split has the early-game advantage in champion power, and has much more range than Protect in the late game, which lacks the tools to close the gap making it an easy target for an immobile enemy team. Protect remains the stronger 5v5 team, and Siege can overestimate their power after the initial exchanges and engage early, only to be destroyed in the fight.

    TIPS
    Siege:
    - Avoid team fights
    - Focus carries with poke
    - Be patient
    - Skill shot accuracy essential
    Protect:
    - Must start fights
    - Play around power spikes
    - Be decisive
    - Use summoner spells to start fights
    - Vision denial key

  • PROTECT > CATCH

    Protect specializes in protecting its members, staying power and dealing high damage, and winning objective fights. This doesn't work in Catch's favor, as he wants to quickly kill a target and engage in a 5v4 fight. If Protect plays unified and methodical, Catch has no way to win in the late game. However, Catch is very strong in the early game and can easily abuse the enemy team if he gains an advantage or, by dividing enemies between lanes with pressure, sets up an ambush, finds one out of position, and eliminates him.

    TIPS
    Protect:
    - Stay grouped
    - Only tanks face check
    - March down mid
    - Be first to objectives
    - Be extremely cautious when split
    Catch:
    - Force skirmishes early and often
    - Be decisive
    - Vision control key
    - Rotate between objectives

  • CATCH > CHARGE

    Catch has the early-game advantage and can easily abuse Charge in early skirmishes, gaining vision and setting up his own ambushes. Charge doesn't have the tools to handle this type of aggression well, and often, if he loses even one team member, his engagement potential is significantly reduced, making him ineffective. However, Charge always has the late-game advantage, for objective clashes, and Catch can overestimate his strength in a 5v5 matchup due to his accumulated advantage and engage, thus playing into the opponent's hands.

    TIPS
    Catch:
    - Force early skirmishes
    - Avoid 5v5 team fights
    - Vision Control
    - Pressure side lanes and look for ambushes on isolate targets
    - Bait out ultimates
    Charge:
    - Stick together
    - Play safe early
    - Mid game team fights
    - Bait Drake/Baron

  • CHARGE > SPLIT

    Charge has the advantage of being able to ignore the stalemate Split wants to implement and force the four Split members onto the objectives; the four of them lack the strength to push them back. Furthermore, Charge has the tools to catch the out-of-position split-pusher and eliminate him. On the other hand, Split tends to have an early-game advantage and win in lanes, a different goal than Charge, which wants the enemies grouped together at the objectives.

    TIPS
    Charge:
    - Camp split pusher early
    - Deny vision
    - Force fights at neutral objectives
    - Dive enemy team
    Split:
    - Funnel esources into split pusher
    - Track enemy team
    - Vision control key
    - Look for steals, not fights

How to make a team

  1. Champion Pool

    Enstablish the team champion pool. Select the best champions for each player divided by tiers.
    S-tier is for champions who you would lock in a tourney's final.
    A-tier is for champions that you feel confident to play.
    Situational is for champions that are good but only in specific scenarios.
    It's important that every one is honest at this.

  2. Team Identity

    Starting from players game style and skill find the overall team identity. Look for the best in each player, what you enjoy to play, find who has carry potential, and who is the shotcaller.

  3. Pick 2 Compositions

    Based on team identiy and total champion pool, choose 2 team compositions to stick with.
    Start from the most obvious one, and than chose its hard counter. This is important because of team compositions match-up system. Doing so you have the possibility to answer well enough to every enemy team, by not have to play against any hard counter if you draft well. Reducing the chances to play only against soft-counters or match-ups in your favor.
    TIP: Based on your team level consider to chose Charge and Protect, because they are the easiest comps to play, and try to avoid Siege and Split because they demand the most game knowledge and coordination.

  4. Fill the gaps

    Now each player trains some usefull champion for the team.
    Because it's not easy to stick 5 players to one identity, it possible that some player have to do some sacrifice and adapt more than the others. It's time to learn some new playstyle and champion archetype based on the archetype table.
    Look at the current patch, and based on your played archetype, look for the best picks in the meta and learn them.
    Plus is a good idea to learn some flex and blind pick, to empower your team draft.

  5. Learn to Draft

    The ban phase is a game before the game. Starting with advantage on the draft can shift the weights to the winning condition.
    B = blue, R = red, 1,2,3,4,5 = first pick, second pick, third pick, forth pick, fifth pick.
    First-Phase Ban
    Blue: Counter to your B1 or Target Ban;
    Red: B1 you don't want to face or Target Ban;
    First-Phase Picks
    B1: Blind and/or Flex Pick
    R1, R2: Counter pick to B1, Blind and/or Flex Pick;
    B2, B3: Counter pick to R1 and R2; if you took a flex pick, try not to break it (ADC), look for synergies with B1, and cover any roles at risk of being banned;
    R3: A pick that rotates the team's identity if it's countered, or a specific counter pick;
    Second-Phase Ban
    Red: Counter to your R4 or Target Ban on a role not yet filled;
    Blue: Possible best R4s, counters to your B4 and B5s, if you have Team Advantage pivot picks;
    Second-Phase Picks
    R4: If you have a flex pick, keep it, try to leave key roles for R5 (Top, Mid, or Support);
    B4, B5: Counter the enemy team's plan or reinforce your own plan. Try to avoid champions with strong counters, and if you can't, pivot to a comp pick.
    R5: A counter pick to the laner or the enemy plan.
    GOLDEN RULE
    Be creative with your picks; there is no objectively better or correct pick.

  6. Master your Game

    Now play your compositions and learn every team and champions match-up. Focus on your game plan, the champions power spikes, the win conditions, how to improve the strengths, and cover the weaknesses. Learn to assign lane match-ups and rotations, when it's time to engage, to disengage, to push, to recall. Plus focus to improve your team comunication, shotcalling and coordination.

  7. Learn & Grow

    To grow as team, after you have mastered your first two team compositions, you could try to add another one and to learn its champion archetypes and playstyle.
    Another way to improve is to as players:
    learn the timers of items, ultimates, jungle camps;
    learn to itemize;
    learn the other champions power spikes and game plan;
    learn to vision control;
    learn to farm;
    learn to pick your fights.
